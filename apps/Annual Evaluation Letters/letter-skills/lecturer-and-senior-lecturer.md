# Letter Skill: Lecturer / Senior Lecturer / Principal Lecturer (APT Faculty)

## Category Description
Academic Professional Track (APT) faculty whose primary responsibility is teaching. NOT evaluated on research. Includes three ranks: Lecturer, Senior Lecturer, and Principal Lecturer. Evaluated on Teaching and Service only.

## Performance Areas Evaluated
- **Teaching**: PRIMARY focus. This is the core responsibility.
- **Service**: Evaluated, but expectations vary by rank (more expected of senior/principal lecturers).
- **Research**: NOT APPLICABLE. Never evaluate APT faculty on research. Never reference the absence of research negatively.

## CRITICAL RULE
**Do NOT evaluate on Research & Publication. Do NOT reference the absence of research activity. Do NOT compare to tenure-track faculty research productivity. Research is simply not part of the APT faculty role.**

If the department head's style explicitly states "Research -- Not applicable" or "N/A in research," include that brief statement. Otherwise, simply omit research entirely.

## Required Letter Sections (in order)

### 1. Opening / Boilerplate
- Thank for Professional Activity Report / Faculty 180 submission
- State evaluation period
- Reference Mays Guidelines and 4-level scale
- Note the assessment basis: reappointment decision and resource allocation
- Note that senior clinical/APT faculty reviewed the report (NOT tenured professors -- the review body is different for APT faculty)

Sean's opening is shorter and more direct for APT faculty than for tenure-track. Rich uses the same 4-purpose boilerplate but notes "senior clinical faculty" or "a collection of faculty" as reviewers.

### 2. Teaching
This is the most substantive section:
- **Courses taught**: Specific course numbers, sections, and student counts
- **Teaching evaluations**: Numerical scores, comparison to department averages
- **Student feedback**: Comments on teaching effectiveness
- **Teaching load**: Note if above standard (overload teaching)
- **Course types**: Note range (large sections, small seminars, online, study abroad, honors)
- **Curriculum contributions**: New course development, textbook adoption, course coordination
- **Student mentoring outside classroom**: Advising student organizations, BUSN 101, study abroad
- **Professional development**: Workshops attended, teaching improvement activities
- **GPR management**: Note if maintaining academic standards while achieving good evaluations
- **Rating statement**: "Based on the Mays Guidelines and your teaching evaluations, I assess your teaching as [rating]."

Key language patterns:
- Sean: "I greatly appreciate your significant contributions to our teaching and student service missions..."
- Sean: "It is truly remarkable given the large size of your course sections and the rigor of the class."
- Rich: Lists accomplishments as bullet points under "Teaching" header, then faculty comments
- Jamie: Includes "Most Positive Comments" and "Most Critical Comments" from students, plus department average vs. individual average comparison

### 3. Service
- Student organization advising
- Committee work (department, college, university)
- Program coordination roles
- Mentoring of students and other instructors
- Community engagement
- For senior/principal lecturers: more substantial service expected and recognized
- **Rating statement**: "Based on the Mays Guidelines, I assess your service as [rating]."

### 4. AACSB Paragraph
This is a distinctive element of APT faculty letters:

**Sean's version**: References AACSB guidelines requiring all faculty to demonstrate activities maintaining currency and relevance of instruction. Encourages proactive professional development. Mentions maintaining instructional practice status for accreditation. Notes CPA license or similar professional credentials if relevant.

**Rich's version**: Includes a full list of AACSB-recognized activities:
- Consulting activities material in terms of time and substance
- Faculty internships
- Development and presentation of executive education programs
- Sustained professional work
- Significant participation in business professional associations
- Relevant, active service on boards of directors
- Documented continuing professional education experiences
- Documented professional certifications in the area of teaching
- Participation in professional events focused on practice of business
- Participation in activities placing faculty in direct contact with business leaders

### 5. Summary / Overall Assessment
- Combined or separate ratings for teaching and service
- Sean typically combines: "Overall, I assess your teaching and service as excellent."
- Rich separates: "N/A in research / Effective in teaching / Excellent in service"
- Jamie separates with "Overall Evaluation" labels per section
- Endorsement of professional goals
- Encouragement for continued development
- Expression of gratitude

### 6. Closing
- Warm appreciation for contributions
- Offer to discuss review
- Contact information for scheduling
- Signature acknowledgment

## Tone Guidance by Rating Level

**Teaching Excellent**: Celebrate the teaching impact. Note specific indicators (evaluations, student counts, course range, student feedback). For senior/principal lecturers, emphasize leadership and mentoring of other instructors.

**Teaching Effective**: Acknowledge solid performance. Encourage continued improvement. Suggest working with experienced instructors. Note specific strengths while identifying growth areas.

**Teaching Needs Improvement**: Be direct about concerns. Reference specific evaluation scores, student feedback, or course organization issues. Provide concrete suggestions for improvement. (See White/Jamie letter for an example of addressing teaching concerns for an APT faculty member.) Note department averages for comparison.

**Service Excellent**: Recognize scope and impact of service contributions. Note leadership roles, program coordination, committee work. Especially important for senior/principal lecturers.

**Service Effective**: Acknowledge appropriate level of engagement. May suggest additional opportunities.

## Rank-Specific Adjustments

### Lecturer
- Newer to the role; more encouragement and developmental guidance
- Service expectations are lower
- Mentoring from experienced instructors encouraged
- Professional development actively encouraged

### Senior Lecturer
- More established; leadership contributions recognized
- Greater service expectations
- May coordinate courses or programs
- Mentoring role toward newer lecturers

### Principal Lecturer
- Most senior APT rank; significant leadership expected
- Program coordination, course leadership, assessment roles
- Extensive service recognized
- May teach across multiple programs and modalities
- Mentoring and institutional knowledge valued

## What NOT to Include
- Do NOT include research evaluation or any reference to research expectations
- Do NOT include tenure trajectory or promotion-to-full language (APT has its own promotion track)
- Do NOT compare to tenure-track faculty
- Do NOT include full professor vote tallies (APT faculty are reviewed by senior APT/clinical faculty, not full professors)
- Do NOT include department peer research rankings
