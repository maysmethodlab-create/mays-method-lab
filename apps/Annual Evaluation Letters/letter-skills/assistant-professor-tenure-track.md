# Letter Skill: Assistant Professor (Tenure-Track)

## Category Description
Untenured tenure-track faculty working toward tenure and promotion to Associate Professor. Evaluated on Research & Publication, Teaching, and Service. Research trajectory is the primary concern.

## Performance Areas Evaluated
- **Research & Publication**: PRIMARY focus. Evaluated over a 3-year rolling window.
- **Teaching**: Important but secondary to research for tenure purposes.
- **Service**: Expected to be limited at this career stage.

## Required Letter Sections (in order)

### 1. Opening / Boilerplate
- Thank the faculty member for submitting their Professional Activity Report (or Faculty 180 submission)
- State evaluation period: January 1, [YEAR] to December 31, [YEAR]
- Reference the Mays Business School Faculty Guidelines
- State the 4-level rating scale (Excellent, Effective, Needs Improvement, Unsatisfactory)
- Note that tenured faculty reviewed the report and vita and provided input

### 2. Research & Publication
This is the most substantive section. Must cover:
- **Publications**: Journals, tier classification (top-tier, A-level, etc.), co-authorship
- **Pipeline**: Papers under review, revise-and-resubmit decisions (note which round and which journal)
- **Working papers**: Count and status
- **Conferences and presentations**: Named conferences, invited seminars at other universities
- **National reputation indicators**: Editorial board memberships, discussant invitations, workshop invitations at peer/aspirant institutions
- **Rating statement**: "Based on the Mays Guidelines, I assess your research performance as [rating]."

Key language patterns observed:
- "It is both notable and exciting that..."
- "It is also very positive that you have a robust pipeline of working papers..."
- "Your research pipeline reflects your strong work ethic and commitment to research excellence."
- For tenure concerns: "Your success in publishing your work in the top scholarly journals will be critical to a positive tenure decision."
- Jamie's style: "Highlights of your research activity during the three-year evaluation window include:" followed by bullet points

### 3. Teaching
- Courses taught (specific course numbers and sections)
- Teaching evaluations (numerical scores if available)
- Student feedback highlights (Jamie includes actual positive and critical student comments)
- Comparison to department averages (Jamie's style)
- Appreciation for investment in students
- Encouragement to work with experienced instructors
- **Rating statement**: "Based on the Mays Guidelines and your teaching evaluations, I assess your teaching as [rating]."

### 4. Service
- Expected to be limited at this career stage -- state this explicitly
- Appropriate service activities: PhD committee, workshop attendance, faculty recruiting, meeting with workshop guests
- Do NOT criticize limited service for untenured faculty
- **Rating statement**: "Based on the Mays Guidelines, I assess your service as [rating]."

### 5. Tenure Trajectory / Tenured Faculty Consensus
This is a critical section unique to assistant professors:
- Report the consensus of the tenured faculty regarding progress toward tenure
- Key phrase: "the consensus of the tenured faculty is that you are on trajectory toward achieving a tenurable record"
- If concerns exist, frame constructively: "your success in publishing your work in the top scholarly journals will be critical to a positive tenure decision"
- Department head concurrence with the committee evaluation
- Endorsement of the faculty member's goals
- Jamie's style includes: mid-term review date, specific guidance on what's needed for a successful tenure case, discussion of independent research ability

### 6. Closing
- Express support and encouragement
- Offer to meet (Sean: "contact Diana Kruse to schedule a time to meet with me"; Jamie: "Please let me know what the department can do to help you meet these goals!")
- Warm personal note (Sean: "I am grateful to be your colleague!"; Jamie: "It is a pleasure to work with you!")
- Signature acknowledgment line

## Tone Guidance by Rating Level

**All areas Excellent**: Celebratory but not over-the-top. Highlight specific achievements. Express genuine excitement about trajectory.

**Research Effective, other areas strong**: Focus on encouragement. Acknowledge solid progress while noting what's needed for tenure. Frame as supportive guidance, not criticism.

**Research Needs Improvement**: This is serious for an assistant professor. Be direct but constructive. Specify what's needed (more publications in top journals, moving papers through pipeline). Mention tenure timeline. Offer support.

**Mixed ratings across areas**: Address each area honestly. Don't let strong teaching compensate for weak research in the overall narrative -- research is the primary tenure criterion.

## What NOT to Include
- Do not include AACSB paragraph (that's for APT faculty only)
- Do not rank against department peers (that's Rich's style for associate professors)
- Do not include full professor vote tallies unless the department head's style calls for it
- Do not reference promotion to full professor (that's for associate professors)

## Style Variations to Match

The AI must adapt to the department head's established style:
- **Sean-style**: Flowing narrative paragraphs, no section headers for assistant professors, ratings stated inline, warm personal tone
- **Rich-style**: Would include vote tallies, bullet-point summaries, peer rankings (no assistant professor templates from Rich yet)
- **Jamie-style**: MEMORANDUM format, bulleted highlights per section, separate "Tenured Faculty Assessment" and "Department Head Assessment" sections, vote tallies, student comments, department average comparisons
