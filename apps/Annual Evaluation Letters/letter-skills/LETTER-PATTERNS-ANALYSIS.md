# Evaluation Letter Patterns Analysis
## Cross-Department Analysis from Template Letters

This document synthesizes structural and tonal patterns observed across 14+ actual evaluation letters from three Mays Business School department heads. It serves as the master reference for per-category letter skill files.

---

## Department Head Writing Styles

### Style A: Sean McGuire (Accounting)
- **Tone**: Warm, personal, narrative-driven. Genuinely collegial.
- **Structure**: Flowing paragraphs (assistant professors) or labeled sections with headers (associate professors). Ratings stated inline within prose.
- **Signature phrases**: "I am very grateful for...", "I am delighted, but not surprised...", "It is both notable and exciting that...", "I am grateful to be your colleague!"
- **Closing pattern**: Encourages goals, offers support, warm sign-off, suggests scheduling via assistant (Diana Kruse).
- **Unique elements**: AACSB paragraph for APT faculty about maintaining currency/relevance. Tenured faculty consensus language for tenure-track. Combined rating statement for APT ("I assess your teaching and service as excellent").

### Style B: Rich Metters (INFO)
- **Tone**: Direct, data-driven, sometimes blunt. Candid about shortcomings.
- **Structure**: Highly structured with distinct sections. Includes full professor vote tallies with exact counts. Ranks faculty against department peers ("ranks 11th among scholars"). Bullet-point accomplishment summaries.
- **Signature phrases**: "I certify your performance according to Mays' standards as...", "If you wish, let us meet and discuss your review."
- **Closing pattern**: Brief, businesslike. Signature acknowledgment box.
- **Unique elements**: Full professor voting tallies (e.g., "Excellent (5), Effective (3)"). Verbatim written comments from faculty. Dean's standard for summer pay mentioned. AACSB life-long learning checklist for APT faculty. Explicit department ranking of research productivity.

### Style C: Jamie Brown (Finance)
- **Tone**: Professional, balanced, thorough. Structured but warmer than Rich's style.
- **Structure**: MEMORANDUM format. Distinct labeled/underlined sections (Research, Teaching, Service). Bulleted highlights of accomplishments. Separate "Tenured Faculty Assessment" narrative section. Separate "Department Head Assessment" section with independent evaluation. Formal vote tallies with counts.
- **Signature phrases**: "It is a pleasure to work with you!", "Thank you very much for your contributions to the Adam C. Sinn '00 Department of Finance and Mays Business School."
- **Closing pattern**: Acknowledges contributions, expresses gratitude, signature acknowledgment.
- **Unique elements**: Includes actual student comments (most positive and most critical) for some letters. Department average vs. individual evaluation average comparison. Promotion/tenure/reappointment section for APT faculty explaining next rank requirements. Uses "three-year evaluation window" for research. AI-facilitated summaries acknowledged in some letters.

---

## Common Elements Across All Department Heads

1. **Mays 4-level rating scale**: All use Excellent, Effective, Needs Improvement, Unsatisfactory
2. **Per-area ratings**: Separate ratings for Teaching, Research, and Service (Research is N/A for APT faculty)
3. **Evaluation period**: January 1 to December 31 of the review year
4. **Three-year window for research**: Research evaluated over a rolling three-year period
5. **Faculty 180/Professional Activity Report**: Referenced as the data source
6. **Tenured/Full professor review**: Tenured faculty review tenure-track colleagues; for APT, senior clinical/APT faculty review
7. **Mays Guidelines reference**: All cite the Mays Faculty Guidelines as the standard
8. **Signature acknowledgment**: All require faculty signature acknowledging receipt (not agreement)

## Patterns by Faculty Category

### 1. Assistant Professor (Tenure-Track)
**Template letters**: Kim, Munevar, Puccia (Sean/Accounting); Cziraki (Jamie/Finance)

**Subject line**: "Annual Review of Progress Towards Tenure" (Sean) or "Annual Performance Review: [dates]" (Jamie)

**Key structural elements**:
- Opening paragraph thanking for Professional Activity Report submission
- Statement of evaluation period and Mays Guidelines reference
- Tenured faculty review and input acknowledged
- Research section: Pipeline discussion (publications, R&Rs, working papers, conference presentations)
- Teaching section: Course evaluations, student feedback, specific courses taught
- Service section: Expected to be limited; appropriate service noted (PhD committee, recruiting, workshops)
- **Tenure trajectory statement**: "The consensus of the tenured faculty is that you are on trajectory toward achieving a tenurable record" (Sean) or detailed assessment of tenure prospects with specific guidance (Jamie)
- **Mid-term review reference** (Jamie): Notes upcoming mid-term review date
- Closing with encouragement and support

**Rating pattern**: Per-area ratings stated inline. For assistant professors, research trajectory is the dominant concern.

**Tone calibration**:
- Predominantly encouraging and supportive
- Research concerns framed constructively ("we strongly encourage you to...")
- Teaching acknowledged as important but secondary to research for tenure case
- Service expectations explicitly limited ("we want your service activities to be limited at this important time")

### 2. Associate Professor (Tenured)
**Template letters**: Kartapanis, Stuber (Sean/Accounting); Agrawal, Arreola-Risa, DJ Lee, Stauffer, Sen, Zhao, Zhang (Rich/INFO); Wu (Jamie/Finance)

**Subject line**: "Annual Review of Progress Towards Tenure" (Sean, appears to be template carryover) or "Annual Review" (Rich) or "Annual Performance Review: [dates]" (Jamie)

**Key structural elements**:
- Sean uses **labeled section headers** (Research, Teaching, Service, Summary) unlike his flowing-paragraph style for assistant professors
- Rich includes **full professor vote tallies** with exact counts and **verbatim written comments**
- Jamie uses **MEMORANDUM format** with bulleted highlights, separate Tenured Faculty Assessment narrative, vote tallies, and independent Department Head Assessment
- **Promotion-to-full discussion**: All three department heads discuss trajectory toward full professor
- Consensus language: "on trajectory toward promotion to full professor" (Sean) or "above trajectory" (Sean for Stuber)
- Rich ranks associate professors against department peers in research productivity

**Rating pattern**: Per-area ratings clearly stated, often with more nuance (e.g., different ratings across areas are common).

**Tone calibration**:
- More substantive and evaluative than assistant professor letters
- Greater emphasis on national/international reputation building
- Service expectations increase ("appropriate that you begin to take on modest service responsibilities")
- Candid feedback on areas needing improvement (especially Rich's style)
- Recognition of contributions to department culture, PhD mentoring, editorial service

### 3. Full Professor
**Template letters**: None currently available

**Expected structure** (based on Mays Guidelines and patterns from other categories):
- Similar to associate professor but without promotion trajectory language
- Focus on sustained excellence and leadership
- Greater weight on service and mentoring contributions
- External reputation and national standing discussed
- Editorial board service, conference leadership, grant activity highlighted

### 4. Lecturer
**Template letters**: Hurta (Sean/Accounting); Curtsinger, Stech, Berberian (Rich/INFO)

**Subject line**: "Annual Review"

**Key structural elements**:
- **NO research section** -- research is explicitly "Not applicable" or simply omitted
- Evaluation focuses exclusively on teaching and service
- AACSB paragraph about maintaining currency/relevance of instruction (Sean) or "life-long learning" checklist (Rich)
- Reappointment decision referenced
- Shorter overall than tenure-track letters
- Senior clinical/APT faculty conduct the review (not tenured professors)
- Sean combines ratings: "I assess your teaching and service as excellent"
- Rich separates: "N/A in research / Effective in teaching / Excellent in service"

**Rating pattern**: Teaching and Service only. Research is N/A. Some department heads combine into one statement, others separate.

**Tone calibration**:
- Warm appreciation for teaching dedication and student impact
- No comparison to research peers
- Professional development and AACSB compliance encouraged
- Service activities valued but not expected to be extensive for lecturers

### 5. Senior Lecturer / Principal Lecturer
**Template letters**: Allen (Sean/Accounting); Boone, Phinney (Rich/INFO)

**Subject line**: "Annual Review"

**Key structural elements**:
- Very similar to Lecturer letters but with:
  - Greater emphasis on leadership roles and program coordination
  - More substantial service expectations acknowledged
  - Teaching volume and impact highlighted (Allen taught 3,000+ students)
  - Mentoring of other instructors noted
  - Committee service more prominent
- AACSB paragraph included
- No research evaluation

**Rating pattern**: Teaching and Service only. Same as Lecturer.

**Tone calibration**:
- Recognizes seniority and leadership contributions
- More weight on program-level impact and mentoring
- Appreciation for flexibility and institutional knowledge

### 6. Executive Professor / Professor of Practice
**Template letters**: Ranzilla (Sean/Accounting); Amos (Jamie/Finance, as Associate Professor of Practice/Executive Associate Professor); White (Jamie/Finance, as Professor of Practice/Executive Professor)

**Subject line**: "Annual Review" (Sean) or "Annual Performance Review: [dates]" (Jamie)

**Key structural elements**:
- **Research formally N/A** but may note intellectual engagement (Ranzilla attending research workshops; White's position "does not have a formal research expectation")
- Teaching evaluation prominent -- industry experience and practical application valued
- Service can be very substantial (Amos's letter details extensive operational and program leadership)
- **Promotion/tenure/reappointment section** (Jamie): Explains next rank in track and minimum promotion requirements
- **Student comments** included (Jamie): Both positive and critical
- **Department average comparison** (Jamie): Individual evaluation scores compared to department averages
- AACSB paragraph (Sean): About maintaining instructional practice status
- Professional activities outside academy noted (White's board service, CPA licenses)

**Rating pattern**: Teaching and Service evaluated. Research N/A or noted as not having formal expectation. Jamie provides "Overall Evaluation" per section.

**Tone calibration**:
- Values industry expertise and real-world perspective
- Appreciates contributions beyond traditional faculty role
- Can be candid about teaching concerns (White's letter addresses course organization issues directly)
- Acknowledges unique value of practitioner perspective

## Critical Rules for AI Letter Generation

1. **Never fabricate accomplishments**. Every specific claim (publications, courses, committees, conference presentations) must come from uploaded source documents.
2. **Match the department head's actual style**. If the DH writes warm narrative paragraphs, don't produce bullet-pointed lists. If the DH uses formal vote tallies, include them.
3. **Per-area ratings must be stated explicitly** in the letter, using the exact Mays 4-level scale.
4. **Research is N/A for APT faculty**. Never evaluate lecturers, senior lecturers, principal lecturers, or professors of practice on research. Never reference the absence of research negatively.
5. **Three-year window for research**. Research is evaluated over the most recent three fiscal years, not just the current year.
6. **Tenure/promotion trajectory language** varies by category. Use "tenurable record" for assistant professors, "promotion to full professor" for associate professors.
7. **AACSB paragraph** appears only in APT faculty letters, not in tenure-track letters.
8. **Signature acknowledgment** should be included, noting that signature does not indicate agreement.
9. **Student comments**: Include only if the department head's style typically includes them (Jamie does; Sean and Rich generally don't for tenure-track).
10. **Department rankings**: Include only if the department head's style uses them (Rich does; Sean and Jamie generally don't).
