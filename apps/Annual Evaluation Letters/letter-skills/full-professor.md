# Letter Skill: Full Professor (Tenured)

## Category Description
Senior tenured faculty at the highest tenure-track rank. Evaluated on Research & Publication, Teaching, and Service. No promotion trajectory to discuss (already at highest rank). Focus shifts to sustained excellence, leadership, and mentoring contributions.

## Performance Areas Evaluated
- **Research & Publication**: Evaluated over a 3-year rolling window. Sustained productivity expected.
- **Teaching**: Full range of courses, graduate and executive programs.
- **Service**: Highest service expectations of any tenure-track rank. Leadership roles, editorial boards, professional society engagement, PhD program stewardship.

## Expected Letter Sections (in order)

### 1. Opening / Boilerplate
- Same as associate professor letters
- Note that evaluation informs resource allocation (no reappointment concern -- full professors have tenure)

### 2. Research & Publication
- Three-year publication record in top journals
- Editorial board service and leadership
- Grant activity
- PhD student mentoring and co-authorship
- Conference keynotes, plenary sessions, or distinguished lectures
- National/international reputation indicators
- **Rating statement**

### 3. Teaching
- Courses taught across programs
- Graduate course development
- PhD seminar instruction
- Teaching evaluations
- Student mentoring impact
- **Rating statement**

### 4. Service
This section is typically more substantial for full professors:
- Editorial board service (editor, associate editor, editorial board member)
- Professional society leadership
- Department and college committee leadership (not just membership)
- PhD program oversight
- Faculty mentoring (junior colleague mentoring)
- External review committee service (promotion and tenure reviews at other universities)
- Dean's advisory roles, search committees
- **Rating statement**

### 5. Summary
- Acknowledge sustained contributions
- Note impact on department culture and reputation
- Recognize leadership and mentoring
- Department head's assessment

### 6. Closing
- Express appreciation
- Offer to discuss
- Signature acknowledgment
