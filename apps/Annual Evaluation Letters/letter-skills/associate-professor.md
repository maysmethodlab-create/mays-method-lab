# Letter Skill: Associate Professor (Tenured)

## Category Description
Tenured faculty working toward promotion to Full Professor. Evaluated on Research & Publication, Teaching, and Service. Greater emphasis on national reputation, editorial service, and mentoring than assistant professors.

## Performance Areas Evaluated
- **Research & Publication**: Evaluated over a 3-year rolling window. Publication quality and volume assessed relative to department peers.
- **Teaching**: Full range of courses expected, including graduate and executive programs.
- **Service**: Greater service expectations than assistant professors. Editorial board service, committee leadership, PhD mentoring, and professional society engagement expected.

## Required Letter Sections (in order)

### 1. Opening / Boilerplate
- Thank for Professional Activity Report / Faculty 180 submission
- State evaluation period
- Reference Mays Guidelines and 4-level scale
- Note that tenured professors / full professors reviewed the report
- Note that this assessment informs departmental resource allocation decisions

### 2. Research & Publication
This section is more evaluative than for assistant professors:
- **Publication count and quality**: Specific journals named, tier classification
- **Three-year publication record**: Compared to department norms
- **Pipeline**: R&Rs, papers under review, working papers
- **Department ranking** (Rich's style): "I judge that your research record ranks [Nth] among the scholars in our department"
- **Dean's standards** (Rich's style): Reference to dean's standard for summer pay (e.g., two articles in five years in dean's top journal list)
- **National reputation**: Editorial board service, conference invitations, reviewing activity
- **PhD student mentoring**: Investment in doctoral students
- **Rating statement**

Key language patterns:
- Sean: "You have a tremendous impact on our department's research culture through your active participation in workshops, investment in PhD students..."
- Rich: "You are a member of a highly productive department. For this three year time period for this review, I judge that your research record ranks [Nth]..."
- Jamie: "You have a strong record of publications in the top and a healthy pipeline of working papers."

### 3. Teaching
- Courses and programs taught (undergraduate, MBA, MS, EMBA, PMBA, online)
- Teaching evaluations with scores
- Student comments (Jamie's style)
- Course development contributions
- Department average comparison
- Range of programs taught recognized as valuable
- **Rating statement**

### 4. Service
Expectations are higher than for assistant professors:
- Workshop series leadership
- Faculty recruiting involvement
- PhD committee service and mentoring
- Editorial board service and journal reviewing
- Professional society engagement (conferences, leadership)
- Department and college committee work
- Curriculum development
- **Rating statement**

### 5. Summary / Promotion Trajectory
- Report full professor consensus on trajectory toward promotion to full professor
- Key phrases: "on trajectory toward promotion to full professor" or "above trajectory toward promotion to full professor"
- Full professors' specific comments referenced (Sean) or quoted verbatim (Rich)
- Department head's independent assessment and concurrence
- Jamie: Vote tallies with counts, followed by Department Head Assessment as separate section
- Endorsement of short-term and long-term goals
- Offer of support

### 6. Closing
- Express appreciation for contributions
- Offer to discuss review
- Warm sign-off
- Signature acknowledgment

## Tone Guidance by Rating Level

**All areas Excellent**: Celebrate exceptional performance. Note contributions across all dimensions. Indicate clear trajectory toward full professor. (See Stuber letter as exemplar.)

**Research Excellent, other areas Effective**: Acknowledge strong research while noting areas for growth in teaching or service. For associate professors, growing service is expected.

**Research Effective or Needs Improvement**: Be direct about expectations for promotion to full. Provide specific guidance on what's needed. Rich is particularly candid here, ranking faculty and comparing to peers. Jamie provides constructive but clear feedback.

**Service Needs Improvement**: Common concern for associate professors. Note the 10% time expectation (Rich). Suggest specific opportunities (editorial service, professional society, department committees).

**Mixed ratings with collegiality concerns**: Handle with care. Note the concern directly but constructively. (See Agrawal letter for an example of addressing collegiality issues while maintaining professionalism.)

## Unique Elements for This Category
- **Full professor vote tallies** (Rich and Jamie styles): Include ratings with vote counts per area plus "As a colleague" dimension
- **Written comments from faculty** (Rich's style): Verbatim quotes from full professor reviews
- **Department peer ranking** (Rich's style): Research productivity ranked against department
- **Dean's summer pay standard** (Rich's style): Two articles in five years in dean's top journal list
- **Promotion timeline context** (Jamie's style): Discussion of when promotion case would come forward

## What NOT to Include
- Do not use "progress towards tenure" language (they are already tenured)
- Do not include AACSB paragraph (that's for APT faculty)
- Do not limit service expectations (unlike assistant professors, associate professors should be taking on more)
