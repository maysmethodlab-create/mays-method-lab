# Letter Skill: Executive Professor / Professor of Practice (APT Faculty)

## Category Description
Academic Professional Track (APT) faculty, typically with significant industry/professional experience. Includes the Professor of Practice track (Assistant Professor of Practice / Associate Professor of Practice / Professor of Practice) and Executive Professor titles. NOT evaluated on research in most cases. Primary focus on teaching and service, with strong emphasis on industry expertise and real-world application.

## Rank Equivalencies (per Mays Guidelines)
- Assistant Professor of Practice = Executive Assistant Professor
- Associate Professor of Practice = Executive Associate Professor
- Professor of Practice = Executive Professor

## Performance Areas Evaluated
- **Teaching**: PRIMARY focus. Industry experience and practical application are distinctive strengths.
- **Service**: Can be very substantial, especially for those in administrative/operational roles.
- **Research**: NOT formally required for most APT appointments. If the faculty member engages in scholarly activity, note it positively but do not evaluate against tenure-track standards.

## Required Letter Sections (in order)

### 1. Opening / Boilerplate
- State the faculty member's exact title and track
- State evaluation period
- Reference Faculty 180 submission
- Reference Mays Guidelines and 4-level scale
- Note that research is not a formal expectation for this position (Jamie: "as with most APT faculty appointments, your position does not have formal research expectations")

### 2. Teaching
- Courses taught with section numbers
- Teaching evaluations with numerical scores
- Student feedback (Jamie includes positive and critical comments)
- Department average comparison (Jamie's style)
- Industry expertise as a teaching strength -- connect academic content to real-world application
- Course development contributions
- If concerns exist: address directly with specific examples and improvement suggestions (see White letter)
- **Overall Teaching Evaluation statement**

### 3. Service
Can be very extensive for this category. May include:
- **Program leadership**: Director of programs, program coordination
- **Department operations**: Administrative oversight, budget management, staff supervision
- **Faculty support**: Appointment letters, teaching assignments, performance evaluation support
- **Student organization advising**
- **Committee service**: Department, college, university level
- **External professional engagement**: Board memberships, industry presentations, professional certifications
- **Strategic initiatives**: Curriculum development, program proposals, donor relations
- **Overall Service Evaluation statement**

### 4. Research and Scholarly Leadership
Brief statement noting that formal research is not expected:
- Sean: Simply omits research section
- Jamie: "As noted above, your position does not have a formal research expectation."
- If the faculty member engages in research workshops or provides research feedback (like Ranzilla), note this as a positive contribution above and beyond expectations

### 5. Promotion, Tenure, and Reappointment (Jamie's style)
This section is distinctive to Jamie's executive professor letters:
- State current rank and track
- Explain the next rank available and minimum promotion requirements
- Note typical time in rank before seeking promotion
- State reappointment decision
- This section helps the faculty member understand their career trajectory within the APT track

### 6. Personal Note / Closing
- Express genuine appreciation for contributions
- Note the distinctive value the faculty member brings (industry perspective, operational leadership, etc.)
- Offer to discuss review
- Signature acknowledgment

## Tone Guidance

**Teaching Excellent + Service Excellent**: Celebrate exceptional performance. Note the distinctive value of industry experience. Highlight breadth of impact. (See Amos and Ranzilla letters.)

**Teaching Needs Improvement**: Be direct and specific. Reference evaluation scores, student feedback, and areas for improvement. Provide concrete suggestions. Acknowledge strengths (industry experience) while noting operational concerns (course organization, communication). (See White letter for an example of a candid Needs Improvement assessment that remains respectful and constructive.)

**Service Excellent (with administrative role)**: Acknowledge the scope of administrative contributions. Note leadership impact. Jamie's Amos letter provides detailed bullet-pointed summary of operational achievements organized by category.

## Unique Elements for This Category
- **Industry expertise is a distinctive strength** -- always acknowledge this
- **Promotion path explanation** (Jamie's style): Explain the APT promotion track requirements
- **Student comments** (Jamie's style): Include most positive and most critical
- **Department average comparison** (Jamie's style): Show individual scores vs. department averages
- **AACSB paragraph** (Sean's style): About maintaining instructional practice status, CPA license, professional credentials
- **Administrative contributions**: May be evaluated on operational and leadership duties beyond traditional faculty role

## What NOT to Include
- Do NOT evaluate research against tenure-track standards
- Do NOT use tenure trajectory language
- Do NOT include full professor vote tallies (unless department head style calls for it)
- Do NOT compare to tenure-track faculty research productivity
