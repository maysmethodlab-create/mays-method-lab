# Staff Evaluation Letter Structure

This is the exact section-by-section structure every staff letter follows. The structure is the same as the faculty skill but with no research/teaching sections and the staff-specific rating + Workday line at the close.

## Document Header (handled by the script)

The TAMU letterhead JPG is embedded by `scripts/create_staff_letter.mjs` on the first page only. You do not include it in the letter text.

## Letter Content Structure

### 1. Date Line
```
[Month] [Year]
```
Example: `May 2026`. Use the month and year the letter is being written.

### 2. MEMORANDUM Header
```
MEMORANDUM
```
Bold. Same font size as the body.

### 3. TO / FROM / SUBJECT Block
```
TO:     [Full Name]
        [Title, Department or Center]

FROM:   [Signer Full Name]
        [Signer Title, Department or School]

SUBJECT: [Year] Performance Evaluation
```

- "TO:", "FROM:", "SUBJECT:" labels are bold; the value text is regular.
- The year in SUBJECT is the year being evaluated.
- For staff at affiliated centers (e.g., CED), the FROM line names the center director, not the dean.

### 4. Salutation
```
Dear [First Name],
```
Use the name they go by, not necessarily their legal first name.

### 5. Opening Paragraph

Pattern (adapt naturally, do not copy verbatim):

"Thank you for serving as [Title] at [Center/Department/Office] this year. I have reviewed your self-evaluation and our discussion notes from your annual performance review. This memorandum is a follow-up to that conversation. In my comments below, I will reference observations from your report and from what you and I have talked through together."

Adjustments:
- If the staff member is new in role, acknowledge the transition.
- If the review covers a partial year (mid-year hire, mid-year review cycle), the opener can mention it but does not need to state precise dates.

### 6. Section: Summary of Major Accomplishments

**Heading**: "Summary of Major Accomplishments" (bold, same size as body text)

3 paragraphs of flowing prose. No bullet points. Common groupings for staff:

- **Paragraph 1 — relationships and presence**: client-facing strength, faculty trust, peer reputation, internal go-to status. Whom does the team turn to, and for what?
- **Paragraph 2 — operational and systems work**: specific tools deployed (e.g., Monday.com), processes built, dashboards or data infrastructure created, courses or programs launched, dollar amounts or volumes if relevant.
- **Paragraph 3 — adaptability under change**: navigating role transitions, office moves, leadership transitions, ambiguity, resource constraints.

Every claim must trace to the source documents. Never invent a number, a date, or a tool name. If the source notes mention "Wharton Certificate course" without specifying anything more, the letter says "Wharton Business Foundations course" only if that exact name is in the source; otherwise just "Wharton Certificate course".

### 7. Section: My Observations and Our Discussion

**Heading**: "My Observations and Our Discussion" (bold)

2-3 paragraphs.

- **Paragraph 1 — overall reflection**: what the year reflects about the person's character and underlying strengths. This is the warmest, most personal paragraph. Use signer-specific phrases the supervisor used in their notes (e.g., "good bones", "flawless coordinating") rather than inventing new language.
- **Paragraph 2 — growth area**: framed constructively as the next step from current strengths. Open by acknowledging where the person is strong, then name what they need to grow into. If the staff member named the same growth area in self-reflection, point that out — "your own self-reflection points in the same direction" — so the criticism reads as shared diagnosis, not external judgment.
- **Paragraph 3 — broader context** (optional): institutional or center-wide issues that frame the staff member's growth area as a shared problem, not solely theirs. Useful when the supervisor's notes contain center-level concerns the staff member echoed.

### 8. Section: Your Plan for the Upcoming Year

**Heading**: "Your Plan for the Upcoming Year" (bold)

**Opening sentence**: "Your professional development plan for the coming year is well-targeted, and it lines up with the growth areas we discussed. The priorities you have identified, and the ones we added together, are:"

**Bullets**: 4-6 items, each 1-2 sentences. Pull directly from:
- The staff member's stated PD needs in the self-evaluation
- The supervisor's PD list from review notes
- Anything the supervisor and staff member added in the review meeting

Frame each bullet in the staff member's own language where possible. Each bullet should name a specific action or focus area, not a vague aspiration.

Example bullets (from Alyssa Morgan letter):
- Complete the Wharton Certificate program in Basics of Business, building the underlying ExecEd commercial fluency we discussed.
- Develop your sales acumen, particularly the consultative-education conversation with prospective and existing clients: contracting, pricing, and how we use Mays faculty as resources.

**Closing sentence**: 1-2 sentences affirming confidence and connecting to the bigger picture.

### 9. Section: Summary

**Heading**: "Summary" (bold)

2-3 paragraphs.

- **Paragraph 1 — personal thanks**: name the specific qualities you are thanking the staff member for. Reference what the year means for the team or the center.
- **Paragraph 2 — quote (optional)**: an inspirational or reflective quote that resonates with the staff member's specific situation. Prefer well-attributed quotes from broadly-known figures (Maya Angelou, Indra Nooyi, well-known authors). Italicize the quote. Connect the quote back to the staff member in 1-2 sentences. Skip this paragraph if no quote fits naturally.
- **Paragraph 3 — formal rating + Workday line**: "Overall, my evaluation is that you **[met expectations / exceeded expectations]** overall. Please ensure you acknowledge the same on Workday. Thank you."

The rating phrase is **bolded**.

### 10. Signature Block
```
________________________________________  ___________________
Signature                                 Date
```

A horizontal underscore line, then "Signature" on the left and "Date" on the right of the next line. The signer's name and title are not repeated here; they are in the FROM block at the top.

---

## Performance Rating Language

Use one of these rating phrases in the Summary section. The phrase is **bolded**:

- **"exceeded expectations"** — for outstanding performance with clear above-expectation impact across multiple areas
- **"met expectations"** — for solid performance meeting role requirements; this is NOT a soft rating, it is the standard for a staff member doing their job well
- **"needs improvement"** — for below-expectations performance; rare, and the rest of the letter must align (do not bury below-expectations findings under generic praise)

The narrative tone of the Summary paragraphs must match the rating. A "met expectations" letter is honest about strengths and growth areas. An "exceeded expectations" letter has more superlatives and named impact. A "needs improvement" letter is direct about the gaps and explicit about the steps required to close them.

## What this structure does NOT include

- Research, scholarship, or publications sections — staff letters do not have these.
- Teaching or doctoral mentoring sections — staff letters do not have these.
- Service-on-committees enumeration — unless the staff role explicitly includes committee staffing or coordination.
- Multi-year scope — staff letters cover a single review period.
- A formal "Goals" header separate from "Plan for the Upcoming Year" — they are the same section.

If a staff member's role overlaps with faculty-adjacent activities (e.g., a research staff member with publications, an instructional designer who teaches), include those in "Major Accomplishments" as a fourth or fifth paragraph but do not create separate research/teaching sections.
