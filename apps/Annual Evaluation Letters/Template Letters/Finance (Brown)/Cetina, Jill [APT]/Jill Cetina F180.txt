Impact Statement (Faculty Evaluation Review)

Teaching: I have restructured two existing courses -- Fixed Income Analysis and Macrofinance -- to bring in my experiences as a central banker, fixed income practiconer and CFA. 
Feedback from students has been positive and that has been rewarding. I have integrated Bloomberg more actively in fixed income as this tool is highly relevant to bonds in the real
world and also tried to get students to use Haver in macrofinance.  I have had several students tell me that my class was their best class at A&M which was highly gratifying.  I care
about my students have sought to both teach and mentor them as they prepare to enter the workforce.
Research: I have a forthcoming paper in Yale's Journal of Financial Crises this spring about lessons from Silicon Valley Bank's failure in 2023.  
Media: I have been quoted in Bloomberg, Reuters, S&P Global, GARP newsletter, CFO dive since joining A&M.  I have also filmed multiple segments with the A&M outreach team which
have aired on tv stations nationally regarding Federal Reserve policy.
Outside activities: I am a recurring op-ed contributor to American Banker -- a national publication that is well read among US bankers; an economic and policy scholar for the
Conference on State Banking Supervisors; faculty and North America director for Bank Treasury Risk Management Certificate; financial advisor for a cancer start up based at The Ohio
State; board member and chair of the audit committee for St. Baldrick's, a large non-profit pediatric cancer foundation.
External lectures: I have received a number of invitations to chair panels or be a keynote speaker.  These are listed in my performance evaluation and promote Texas A&M in national
settings.
Internal lectures: I have spoken to the board of the Commercial banking program, Professor Adam's trading class in the fall (2x), Misty Page's MSF and MSQF students and Aggie
Women in Finance.  Feedback from Misty from my Feb 2025 lecture was super gratifying --  ''Hey Jill,  Thanks so much for your talk last week.  I counted about 15 questions during the
session and afterward, MANY students said how much they enjoyed your talk.  For an online session where your camera wasn’t working, you had your work cut out for you, but your
content was very engaging.  One student said you were the smartest professor they had ever had in college.  I won’t say who since they are in your class now.  S  Thank you again,
and so glad your part of the finance department!  Misty"
I am sure that I have opportunities to improve in the coming academic year.
