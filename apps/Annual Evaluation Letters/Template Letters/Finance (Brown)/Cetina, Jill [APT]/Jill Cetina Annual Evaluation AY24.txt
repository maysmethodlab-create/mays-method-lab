SUBJECT: Annual Performance Review: July 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024 
 
Typically, performance reviews cover a calendar year; however, as you began working for the University on July 1, 2024, this 
review is for the period July 1, 2024 through December 31, 2024. My evaluation is based on the information submitted through 
Faculty 180, which includes documentation of your teaching performance and service activities (as with most APT faculty 
appointments, your position does not have formal research expectations). 
 
Mays Faculty Promotion and Tenure Guidelines define four levels of performance for the key dimensions across which 
performance is reviewed. These levels of performance are:  
• Excellent performance: a high level of performance that meets and exceeds norms and expectations and that is 
reflected by substantive indicators of excellence;  
• Effective performance: performance that meets norms and expectations and that is reflected by substantive 
indicators of effective performance;  
• Needs improvement performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of effective performance 
as reflected by substantive indicators of needs improvement performance;  
• Unsatisfactory performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of excellent, effective, and 
needs improvement performance as reflected by substantive indicators of unsatisfactory performance. 
Teaching: 
Your formal teaching assignments included FINC 501, and FINC 502 (2 sections). You delivered high-impact instruction 
across both undergraduate and graduate fixed income courses, thoughtfully integrating Bloomberg and Haver Analytics to 
connect academic material with real-world application. Your redesign of course content to reflect your extensive 
professional experience was well-received by students, several of whom described your class as the best they had taken at 
Texas A&M. In addition to your formal teaching, your guest lectures and mentorship engagements with student 
organizations and internal programs greatly enriched the learning environment and student career preparation. 
Student feedback on your teaching and impact has been very positive, with many students indicating that you are an 
engaging and highly knowledgeable instructor who brings passion and real-world application to the classroom, making 
complex material accessible and memorable. Others noted some concerns about assessment timing and instructional 
pacing, suggesting that the advanced level of content delivery could occasionally feel overwhelming. Overall, students 
appreciated your depth of expertise and your efforts to connect course concepts to current financial events.

Service: 
 
Your service to the Adam C. Sinn ’00 Department of Finance includes your involvement and leadership with the following 
activities: 
• Associate Director, Commercial Banking Program

You provided outstanding service to the department, college, and broader profession through your applied research, 
media presence, and leadership in regulatory and nonprofit organizations. Your forthcoming article in the Yale Journal of 
Financial Crises and recurring contributions to American Banker have significantly elevated the visibility of our 
department. Your involvement with the Conference of State Banking Supervisors, BTRM, and the St. Baldrick’s Foundation

advancing both the academic and professional mission of the department and Texas A&M. 
 
Overall Evaluation: Excellent 
 
Research and Scholarly Leadership: 
 
As noted above, your position does not have a formal research expectation.  However, we are deeply grateful for your 
outstanding contributions to applied research and thought leadership in financial regulation and macroprudential policy. 
Your ability to translate complex regulatory topics into accessible, policy-relevant insights has enhanced the visibility of the 
Department of Finance and Mays Business School. Through your frequent op-eds in American Banker, media contributions 
to national outlets, and participation in high-profile panels and conferences, you have elevated Texas A&M’s presence in 
national conversations on financial stability. Your work not only informs public discourse but also brings valuable real-
world perspective into the classroom, enriching the academic experience for our students. Thank you for your continued 
dedication and impact!
