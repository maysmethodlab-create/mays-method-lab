SUBJECT: Annual Performance Review: January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024 
 
This letter serves as your performance review for the period January 1, 2024 through December 31, 2024. My evaluation is 
based on the information submitted through Faculty 180, which includes documentation of your teaching performance, 
research productivity, and service activities. 
Mays Faculty Promotion and Tenure Guidelines define four levels of performance for the key dimensions across which 
performance is reviewed. These levels of performance are:  
• Excellent performance: a high level of performance that meets and exceeds norms and expectations and that is 
reflected by substantive indicators of excellence;  
• Effective performance: performance that meets norms and expectations and that is reflected by substantive 
indicators of effective performance;  
• Needs improvement performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of effective performance 
as reflected by substantive indicators of needs improvement performance;  
• Unsatisfactory performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of excellent, effective, and 
needs improvement performance as reflected by substantive indicators of unsatisfactory performance. 
Teaching: 
You taught 11 sections across six different courses, reaching over 1,100 students through a mix of in-person and online 
formats. Your instructor effectiveness scores ranged as high as 5.0/5.0, with an overall average of 4.54/5.0. In addition to 
your core teaching, you led supplemental programs such as the AmplifyU Finance Seminars for student-athletes and the 
ARGUS Enterprise Certification Program for Master of Real Estate students. You supervised 1 Ph.D. and 21 graduate 
students—many outside Mays—which highlights your cross-campus engagement and mentorship. You also taught and 
oversaw internships and experiential learning programs, further enriching student opportunities. 
Your formal teaching assignments included FINC 210 (2 sections), FINC 371 (4 sections), FINC 475 (2 sections) and FINC 
670 . Your comments were generally positive, with many students indicating that you are an engaging, caring, and 
knowledgeable professor who fosters a positive learning environment and encourages student success. Concerns centered 
on exam weight, feedback accessibility, and occasional lack of focus in lectures.

Overall Evaluation: Excellent 
Service: 
 
In 2024, you served as Director of Undergraduate Real Estate Programs and Interim Director of the Master of Real Estate 
program. You founded and advised the Aggie Real Estate Club, which grew to 213 members with over 450 students on its 
communication list. You also served on numerous college-level committees, including curriculum redesign and 
undergraduate/graduate curriculum. Your student mentoring extended beyond Mays through supervision of 
interdisciplinary graduate and doctoral students. 
 
We appreciate your continued service as Director of Undergraduate Real Estate, particularly your work in launching and 
advising the Aggie Real Estate Club, which has quickly become one of our most active student organizations. At the same 
time, we are entering a period of review regarding the structure and responsibilities of all ‘Director of Undergraduate...’ 
leadership roles. As such, we are reappointing you to this position for one additional year, with the understanding that we 
will reassess the need for and scope of the role at the end of the term. 
 
As we move forward, it is essential that this position take on a more proactive and visible role in shaping the 
undergraduate curriculum and coordinating efforts across the undergraduate real estate experience. We also ask that you 
engage more directly in leadership and planning for the undergraduate club’s programming to ensure alignment with 
broader departmental goals. We are hopeful that with focused attention in these areas, we can continue to build on 
recent momentum and strengthen the undergraduate experience for our students. 
 
Overall Evaluation: Effective 
 
Research and Scholarly Leadership: 
 
The research expectations for Clinical faculty members vary based on individual preferences and departmental needs. In 
addition to teaching responsibilities, Clinical faculty are expected to engage in a combination of service and research 
activities. A Clinical faculty who actively engages in research would be expected to have a lower level of service activity, 
and vice versa.   
  
Promotion, Tenure, and Reappointment

September 2021. We are aware you will be seeking promotion to Clinical Professor for the coming term.
