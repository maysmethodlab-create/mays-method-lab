SUBJECT: Annual Performance Review: January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024 
 
Both Texas A&M University and Mays Business School require that the performance of all faculty be reviewed on an 
annual basis.  As noted in the Mays Faculty Promotion and Tenure Guidelines, the purposes of the annual performance 
review include: 
• Creating a sound and logical basis for merit compensation recommendations 
• Providing evaluative feedback regarding how well the individual is currently performing 
• Providing developmental feedback regarding areas where the faculty member’s contribution may be 
enhanced and/or improved in the future 
• Providing feedback regarding progress toward promotion and/or tenure as relevant 
 
Further, the Mays Faculty Promotion and Tenure Guidelines define four levels of performance for the key dimensions 
across which performance is reviewed. These levels of performance are: 
• Excellent performance: a high level of performance that meets and exceeds norms and expectations and that is 
reflected by substantive indicators of excellence; 
• Effective performance: performance that meets norms and expectations and that is reflected by substantive 
indicators of effective performance; 
• Needs improvement performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of effective 
performance as reflected by substantive indicators of needs improvement performance; 
• Unsatisfactory performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of excellent, effective, and 
needs improvement performance as reflected by substantive indicators of unsatisfactory performance. 
 
This letter serves as your performance review for the 2024 calendar year. The following reflects information provided in 
your  Faculty  180  submission.  In  addition, the  tenured  Finance  faculty  had  the  opportunity to  review  your  annual  report 
(containing detailed information about your research, teaching, and service) and current resume and provided me with their 
assessment and feedback.  In the sections below, I first briefly summarize the main contributions you have made in each 
area, present the assessment of the tenured faculty, and provide my own independent assessment.

Research 
Highlights of your research activity during the three-year evaluation window include: 
• In the last three years, you had one paper published in the Brookings Papers on Economic Activity and received one 
revise and resubmit from American Economic Review, a leading economics journal. 
• You are producing a handbook chapter for Entrepreneurship and Innovation Policy and the Economy, Volume 5. 
• You produced four working papers and have started several promising projects 
• You are presenting your papers at influential economics forums and conferences. 
 
Teaching 
• You taught three sections of undergraduate (FINC 381) and three sections of graduate (FINC 642) Money and 
Capital markets Course. 
 
Service   
• You are refereeing for numerous high-quality journals. 
• Member of the program committee for the department’s Young Scholars Finance Consortium. 
• Serving as discussant and session chair at prestigious conferences. 
• Your invited media appearances provide you and your research with a wide exposure. 
 
Tenured Faculty Assessment 
As  you  will see  below,  all nine  members  of the  tenured  faculty  rated  your research performance as “excellent”. We  are 
pleased with your pipeline and with the fact that you are collaborating with your faculty colleagues in the department and 
on mainstream finance research topics. We hope you will show research leadership and consider producing sole authored 
work. You have hit the ground running and we encourage you to continue on this pace. 
 
Ratings (votes) from Tenured Faculty 
Research: Excellent (9) 
Teaching: Excellent (3), Effective (6) 
Service: Effective (9) 
As a colleague: Excellent (9) 
 
Additional Comments from Tenured Faculty 
• You are recognized as an asset to the department. 
• Continue the research trajectory you are on. We are pleased to have you as a colleague. 
 
Department Head Assessment 
I agree with the assessment of the tenured faculty. You had an excellent final year as a visiting assistant professor at TAMU, 
and  we  are  extremely  glad  to  welcome  you  as  a  more  permanent  member  of  the  department.  Your  research  program  is 
important and timely and policy-relevant, and your ability to hit the top economics journals is truly impressive. You have a 
strong pipeline of working papers are diversifying your set of co-authors. You are already establishing a reputation as one 
of the thought leaders on issues related to ‘public R&D’, and, although I agree that you will need to demonstrate research
