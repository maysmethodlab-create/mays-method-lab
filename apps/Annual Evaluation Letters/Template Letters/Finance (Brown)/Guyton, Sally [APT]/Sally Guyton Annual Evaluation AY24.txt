SUBJECT: Annual Performance Review: January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024 
 
This letter serves as your performance review for the period January 1, 2024 through December 31, 2024. My evaluation is 
based on the information submitted through Faculty 180, which includes documentation of your teaching performance, 
and service activities (as with most APT faculty appointments, your position does not have formal research expectations). 
Mays Faculty Promotion and Tenure Guidelines define four levels of performance for the key dimensions across which 
performance is reviewed. These levels of performance are:  
• Excellent performance: a high level of performance that meets and exceeds norms and expectations and that is 
reflected by substantive indicators of excellence;  
• Effective performance: performance that meets norms and expectations and that is reflected by substantive 
indicators of effective performance;  
• Needs improvement performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of effective performance 
as reflected by substantive indicators of needs improvement performance;  
• Unsatisfactory performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of excellent, effective, and 
needs improvement performance as reflected by substantive indicators of unsatisfactory performance. 
Teaching: 
You taught FINC 341 (multiple large sections including Honors) and IBUS 456 during Spring and Fall 2024, reaching over 
1,700 students across sections. These courses are foundational to our Core Body of Knowledge, making your role 
especially impactful. You also led honors projects and mentored students through complex investment analyses. Your 
additional instructional efforts included managing teaching assistants and engaging students through multimedia, self-
check quizzes, and applied finance problems. 
You continue to make a substantial impact on our undergraduate programs through your instruction in FINC 341 and IBUS 
456, including Honors sections. Your commitment to student engagement, mentorship, and managing large enrollment 
sections contributes meaningfully to the success of our core curriculum. 
Your formal teaching assignments included FINC 341 (7 sections). Student evaluation comments in both your large-section 
and Honors were mixed, with some students indicating that you are a passionate and interactive instructor who fosters a 
positive learning environment and provides valuable real-world connections. Others expressed concerns about course 
pacing, organization, and timely feedback, particularly in large sections where students felt a lack of clarity and depth in 
some course content.  We acknowledge that large-section CBK classes should be evaluated against classes of similar size.   
Most Positive Comments  
• Sally Guyton was a wonderful and interactive professor. I do not think there is a single bad thing anyone can 
say about her.

Most Critical Comments

Overall Evaluation: Effective 
Service: 
 
You serve as the advisor to the Financial Management Association (FMA), supporting student development through 
speakers, socials, and annual trips to Austin, Houston, and New York. Additionally, you lead annual study abroad programs 
through the Center for International Business Studies (CIBS), designing and executing international itineraries with a career 
development focus. You also teach and mentor in the FINC 341 Honors sections and manage a large team of TAs. 
 
Your longstanding service to the department and university is exceptional. From advising FMA and leading impactful study 
abroad programs to mentoring Honors students and coordinating TAs, you have demonstrated a deep commitment to 
enriching the student experience well beyond the classroom. 
 
Overall Evaluation: Excellent
