Impact Statement (Faculty Evaluation Review)

1. Conducting study abroad programs is letting the students truly realize that they are part of an entire world, not just the U.S.. They begin to think globally, and are introduced to
other countries' concerns and beliefs. Visiting Europe and Australia/NZ has definitely made the students more aware of protecting our planet.
2. I teach business finance but I weave into the class enough personal finance to help the students have the skills they need to succeed as an adult and be a contributor to our
economy. I always focus on tax laws, what they were and what they are now, so the students can think about what they believe should be in the tax code.  I also bring in current
events OFTEN that relate to the material of the course.
3. I believe FMA should serve two goals: 1) to help the students realize all of the amazing paths that a finance major can take for a career, and 2) to enable the students to create a
network of college friends so that they can help each other throughout their careers and serve as sounding boards for exciting new ideas, etc...
