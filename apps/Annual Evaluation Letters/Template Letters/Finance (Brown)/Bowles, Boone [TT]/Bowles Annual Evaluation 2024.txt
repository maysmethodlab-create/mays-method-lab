SUBJECT: Annual Performance Review: January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024 
 
Both Texas A&M University and Mays Business School require that the performance of all faculty be reviewed on an 
annual basis.  As noted in the Mays Faculty Promotion and Tenure Guidelines, the purposes of the annual performance 
review include: 
• Creating a sound and logical basis for merit compensation recommendations 
• Providing evaluative feedback regarding how well the individual is currently performing 
• Providing developmental feedback regarding areas where the faculty member’s contribution may be 
enhanced and/or improved in the future 
• Providing feedback regarding progress toward promotion and/or tenure as relevant 
 
Further, the Mays Faculty Promotion and Tenure Guidelines define four levels of performance for the key dimensions 
across which performance is reviewed. These levels of performance are: 
• Excellent performance: a high level of performance that meets and exceeds norms and expectations and that is 
reflected by substantive indicators of excellence; 
• Effective performance: performance that meets norms and expectations and that is reflected by substantive 
indicators of effective performance; 
• Needs improvement performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of effective 
performance as reflected by substantive indicators of needs improvement performance; 
• Unsatisfactory performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of excellent, effective, and 
needs improvement performance as reflected by substantive indicators of unsatisfactory performance. 
 
This letter serves as your performance review for the 2024 calendar year. The following reflects information provided in 
your Faculty 180 submission. In addition, the tenured Finance faculty had the opportunity to review your annual report 
(containing detailed information about your research, teaching, and service) and current resume and provided me with 
their assessment and feedback.  In the sections below, I first briefly summarize the main contributions you have made in 
each area, present the assessment of the tenured faculty, and provide my own independent assessment.

Research 
Highlights of your research activity during the three-year evaluation window include: 
• A paper accepted for publication at the Journal of Finance, arguably the most prestigious outlet in finance.  
• A favorable revise and resubmit at the Journal of Financial Economics. 
• You completed multiple working papers that have been accepted for presentation at major conferences. 
• You won a best paper award at the Southwest Finance Association conference. 
 
Teaching 
• Excellent teaching across multiple preps. You developed a new class for the new Flex Online MBA program, in 
addition to continuing to teach an excellent class for the full-time program. 
• You served on three PhD student dissertation committees. 
 
Service   
• Faculty advisor of the business fraternity. 
• Faculty advisor of a student men’s organization. 
• Ad-hoc referee for high quality finance journals. 
 
Tenured Faculty Assessment 
As you will see below, all the of tenured faculty who reviewed your materials rated your research performance as excellent.  
Last year, we observed that you needed to step up your efforts to get papers submitted more quickly. You seem to have 
taken our advice to heart, and we are happy to see that you got a paper accepted at the Journal of Finance and received an 
R&R  at  the Journal  of  Financial  Economics. We  also  are  encouraged  that  you  have  been  submitting  papers  with  some 
frequency. We urge you to continue doing so, and if possible, to increase the rate at which you submit papers.  
In addition, to earn tenure, it is necessary that you demonstrate an independent ability to publish in leading journals. All of 
the  papers  you  have  had  accepted  or  for  which  you  received  an  R&R  are  with  senior  coauthors.  It  is  essential  that  you 
demonstrate your ability to publish papers in leading journals that are either sole-authored or with coauthors at your level 
of seniority or lower. We see you plan to submit a paper authored with current or former TAMU Ph.D. students soon. We 
encourage you to prioritize this project.  
The tenured faculty also unanimously rated your teaching as excellent. You taught an MBA class and a BBA class. Your 
teaching  evaluations  indicate  a  high  degree  of  student  satisfaction. We  also  see no  evidence  to suggest the  high  student 
satisfaction is coming at the expense of rigor, so we have every reason to believe you are teaching excellent courses. We 
further recognize the effort you put into developing a new class for the Flex Online MBA program. While we appreciate 
your achievements in teaching, we think that going forward you should budget relatively more time for research. 
We are happy to see that for service, you are contributing the amount that is expected of an assistant professor on the tenure 
track. For this reason, the tenured faculty unanimously rated your service as satisfactory.

Department Head Assessment 
I agree with the assessment of the tenured faculty. You had an excellent year. Congratulations on seeing the JF in print and 
getting the new R&R at the JFE! You are in good shape for your mid-term review in Fall 2025, but of course your mandatory 
review in 2027-28 will be here before you know it. I fully support your (aggressive) goal of getting three of your completed 
working papers across in the finish line, including the JFE paper. If there is something the department can do to help you 
achieve this goal, I hope you will let me know! And while your primary focus should be on publishing your existing polished 
papers in the best outlets possible, please note the feedback from senior faculty on demonstrating an independent ability to 
publish  in the  leading  journals.  Toward  this  end,  I encourage you  try  to  keep  the  project  you  are  working on  with  PhD 
students moving forward this year. 
Your teaching contributions are exceptional, particularly for an untenured assistant professor.  
Thank you very much for your contributions to the Adam C. Sinn ’00 Department of Finance and Mays Business School. 
It is a pleasure to work with you!
