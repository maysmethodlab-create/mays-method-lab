My most important goal for 2024 was to make significant progress on the revision of "The Market for CEOs" (with Dirk Jenter, LSE) to resubmit it to the Journal of Finance as
soon as possible. 
We have completed all of the empirical work and have written approximately 75% of the response to the referees. The last part is a bit more challenging because it involves
some theoretical arguments raised by one of the referees.
My co-author had some personal issues that prevented him from working on research in the summer of 2024. 
Learning from the past, we tried to set more meetings and milestones that we need to hit so that we have a clear path to resubmission. Based on the progress we made and
my co-author's teaching and other commitments, I expect that we will resubmit by the end of the academic year, around July 2025.
Spring
2023
Ongoing
Work in progress: I currently have three areas with preliminary work. 
1) Local newspaper closures as a source of changes to investor information sets. The second paper on this research agenda, joint work with Jasmin Gider (Tilburg
University), Jordi Mondria (University of Toronto), and my former PhD student, Alison Taylor (Queen's University) is shaping up well. We expect to have a draft on SSRN by
the end of May, 2025. Note that two out of four authors are currently on parental leave, so progress is a bit slower, but is mostly in line with our expectations. 
2) Explicit stakeholder contracts (with Freda Fang, a PhD student at the University of Toronto and Pat Akey, INSEAD): We are collecting data on material contracts between
the firm (management) and various stakeholders such as major shareholders, creditors, and the government. We hope to understand how these contracts tie the hands of
management and the implications for setting managerial incentives.
We expected to finish the data collection and see some initial results by the end of 2024. Progress has been slightly slower because the filings were harder to parse than we
initially thought. We now think that we can collect all the required data by the end of Summer 2025.
3) Performance signals in managerial labor markets (with Dirk Jenter, LSE): we hope to study managerial careers to understand how boards may extract performance signals
in hiring decisions, and whether their evaluation may be biased in some cases.
We expected to make progress on the data collection by the end of 2024. This project was slowed because of some personal issues my co-author was facing. However, we
realized that we could use a commercially available data source for a large part of this project. LSE is in the process of buying that data. Once we have the data, likely in
April 2025, I can make a more precise prediction on the project timeline.
Spring
2023
Ongoing
"Credentials Matter, but Only for Men: Evidence from the S&P 500" (with Adriana Robertson, University of Chicago): we were hoping to submit this paper to a journal for the
first time. 
We hoped to send the paper to a journal in 2024. We updated the data all the way to 2022 (instead of ending in 2018), and added a few more robustness tests in response
to feedback we received at conferences. 
My co-author presented it at the European Finance Association and the American Law and Economics Association. I presented it at the American Economic Association and
the Lone Star Finance Symposium. 
Both of us are teaching in Spring 2025, but we should be able to submit the paper very shortly after we are done. Most of my teaching has already ended, so I am hopeful
that we can submit in May 2025.

Teaching: 
My goal for Spring 2024 was been to solicit and listen attentively to student feedback throughout my teaching. 
In Fall 2024, I made adjustments to my teaching as necessary based on the feedback that I receive.
I believe that I achieved the majority of my teaching goals.
For 2025, I did not make any changes to the FTMBA as the 2024 evaluations were quite encouraging. 
Following advice I received from mentors Shane and Wei, as well as an accounting faculty member, Mary Léa McAnally, who has taught accounting in the PMBA, I have been
making some minor changes to my PMBA teaching. These include fine-tuning the delivery of the material and incorporating more interaction with the students and some
group work.
Spring
2023
Ongoing
Research goals overall: 
Following Shane's advice, I tried to focus most of my efforts on my papers that are R&Rs or are closest to being submitted. Doing so led to steadier progress on my more
advanced projects and slowed progress on my early-stage projects. 
As a result, I had one new R&R (JFQA) and one new working paper. I elaborate on the various elements of my research portfolio in separate line items.
Spring
2024
Ongoing
New "revise and resubmit":
My paper with Adriana Robertson (Chicago) titled "Continuity and Change on Corporate Boards" received an R&R from the Journal of Financial and Quantitative Analysis in
March 2025. 
We started submitting this paper to journals in Fall 2024, and, in accordance with my overall approach to the research portfolio, I focused some of my efforts since then on
making changes as needed and sending it out to (the next) journals.
Looking at the JFQA report, I believe that we should be able to revise the paper by the end of calendar year 2025. Both of our teaching is concentrated in the Spring term,
but I expect good progress once Adriana also finishes most of her courses.
Fall 2024Ongoing
New working paper: 
Last year, I wrote that the second paper from the newspaper closure research agenda would be available in 2024. This prediction turned out to be accurate. Jasmin Gider
(Tilburg) and I wrote "Newspaper Closures and Trading in Local Stocks" and posted it in November 2024. I presented this paper four times in Fall 2024. I will also be
presenting it at four conferences in Spring 2025. My co-author and I believe that the conference acceptances and the SSRN download numbers are an encouraging start.
