 Goals (Faculty Evaluation Review)

FULFILL THE VISION: To fulfill the vision laid out by our forebears at Texas A&M since our founding in 1876, we must do something that truly moves the needle. Being
another excellent fund does not move the needle, we want to change the world and see our benchmarks as global. Cutting-edge research out of the Sinn Center for
Investment Management / Dept of Finance, track record vs benchmark, great student placement and college-wide impact are expected. Big picture we want to produce
titans of Wall Street, billionaires, responsible citizens, and world leaders. It is difficult to benchmark year to year but over decades the results should be obvious. We have
30 years of experience with Aggies on Wall Street as a reference and as something to now harvest. 
How we define success or moving the needle is not limited to money, however we equally value those who chose a family for a season (like myself) or professions such as
teaching 6th grade (this happened) which are incredibly impactful. Above all else, we want to produce students/alumni of high character and caliber. 
Fall 2021Ongoing
SANITY: There are four legs on this stool:
1. Run a Multi-Strat Money Management Firm
2. Teach classes
3. Alumni and External Relations
4. Build the Adam C. Sinn '00 Center for Investment Management
Each of these mandates is a full-time job by itself. We want to do all four well which creates an intense positive feedback loop for Texas A&M. All of that without burning out
or driving my family crazy.
Fall 2021Ongoing
SELFLESS SERVICE: we want to do things TODAY that someone else will take credit for and get paid for 20 years from now. That selfless service and vision is what has
driven A&M from humble beginnings to the great university it is today.
Fall 2021Ongoing
WINNING: Texas A&M should be home to the #1 student-managed fund complex in the world. Our students and alumni should win on the playing field, the board room, C-
Suite, trading floor, symphony hall, art gallery, in the community and at home with their families. Our most valuable input is the culture which facilitates our students /
former students to accomplish this.
Fall 2021Ongoing
BUILD: The Sinn Center for Investment Management is just getting started! We are doing many excellent things. The goal is to maintain the excellence and bolt on new
centers of excellence. The (soon to be renamed) Hedge Fund class has just started in earnest with Sorin and I as co-professors after two beta test classes. The amount of
work on the back end setting up accounts and structuring the investment strategies is just enormous. This will take several years to get to where it needs to be. Remember
Texas A&M is the only school that we know of attempting to run an inhouse hedge fund. Everything we do is greenfield. Sorin and I have put together an excellent board
which will requires ongoing time and work. We have been hosting former student events in Dallas, Houston, NYC, and Europe. The only way this works to it’s full potential is
to have heavy alumni involvement. Splitting Aggies on Wall Street into a Banking Track and a Capital Markets Track in Spring 2026 will further push my time to the limit.
Our goal for 2025 is to hire another instructor to split Rev Fund teaching so I can spend more time on the Sinn (Tanner) Fund Class.
