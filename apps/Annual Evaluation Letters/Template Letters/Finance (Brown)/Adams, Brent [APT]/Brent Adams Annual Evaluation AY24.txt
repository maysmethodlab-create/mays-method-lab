SUBJECT: Annual Performance Review: January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024 
 
This letter serves as your performance review for the period January 1, 2024 through December 31, 2024. My evaluation is 
based on the information submitted through Faculty 180, which includes documentation of your teaching performance and 
service activities (as with most APT faculty appointments, your position does not have formal research expectations). 
Teaching: 
Your formal teaching assignments included FINC 425 (2 sections), FINC 448 (2 sections), and FINC 648 (2 sections). In these 
assignments, your evaluations were positive and exceeded the department averages. The written comments were highly 
positive, with many students indicating you were among the best professors they’ve had at Texas A&M University!  
Most Positive Comments: 
• The most academically and financially enriching experience I’ve had... WELL worth it.  
• BR is one of the best professors at Texas A&M – the Rev Fund course is unparalleled.  
• Taking this class again next semester for zero credit hours just so I can be here again. 
 
Most Critical Comments: 
• There is little organization in this course.  
• Hard to keep track of assignments on Teams.  
• Sometimes felt too much was expected without clear structure. 
 
Department Average (Effective Course/Effective Instructor): 4.40/4.44 
Evaluation Average (Effective Course/Effective Instructor): 4.91/5.0 
 
 
Overall Evaluation: Excellent 
Service: 
 
You’ve made an incredible impact this year through your vision, energy, and deep commitment to the department. From 
building the Adam C. Sinn ’00 Center for Investment Management to expanding Aggies on Wall Street, your efforts are 
positioning Texas A&M as a global leader in student-managed investment education. Beating the University of Texas’ 
Longhorn Fund three years in a row with the Reveille Fund is an outstanding achievement and a point of pride for the 
entire university. 
 
Overall Evaluation: Excellent 
 
Research and Scholarly Leadership: 
 
As noted above, your position does not have a formal research expectation.
