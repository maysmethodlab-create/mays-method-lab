SUBJECT: Annual Performance Review: January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024 
 
This letter serves as your performance review for the period January 1, 2024 through December 31, 2024. My evaluation is 
based on the information submitted through Faculty 180, which includes documentation of your teaching performance, 
research productivity, and service activities. 
Mays Faculty Promotion and Tenure Guidelines define four levels of performance for the key dimensions across which 
performance is reviewed. These levels of performance are:  
• Excellent performance: a high level of performance that meets and exceeds norms and expectations and that is 
reflected by substantive indicators of excellence;  
• Effective performance: performance that meets norms and expectations and that is reflected by substantive 
indicators of effective performance;  
• Needs improvement performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of effective performance 
as reflected by substantive indicators of needs improvement performance;  
• Unsatisfactory performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of excellent, effective, and 
needs improvement performance as reflected by substantive indicators of unsatisfactory performance. 
 
Teaching: 
You taught a broad range of undergraduate and graduate courses in 2024, covering core commercial banking, financial 
sales, and internship supervision. Across all classes, you maintained a strong connection between academic content and 
real-world application. You’ve also contributed through guest lectures and mentoring, and your involvement in internship 
oversight and student advising reflects your deep investment in student development.  
Your formal teaching assignments included FINC 463, FINC 464, FINC 634 and FINC 633. Student participation in your 
course evaluations was low, but student feedback was positive and in line with department averages. I realize it is 
challenging to improve student engagement in the evaluation process, but you might consider trying to increase student 
participation in the evaluation process in the coming year. 
Department Average (Effective Course/Effective Instructor): 4.40/4.44 
Evaluation Average: 4.41/4.52 
 
 
Overall Evaluation: Effective 
Service:

long-term sustainability by balancing external engagement with the academic foundation that supports our students’ 
development. The heart of what we do is still grounded in the student experience, through strong academics, mentorship,
