SUBJECT: Annual Performance Review: January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024 
 
This letter serves as your performance review for the period January 1, 2024 through December 31, 2024. My evaluation is 
based on the information submitted through Faculty 180, which includes documentation of your teaching performance and 
service activities (as with most APT faculty appointments, your position does not have formal research expectations). 
Mays Faculty Promotion and Tenure Guidelines define four levels of performance for the key dimensions across which 
performance is reviewed. These levels of performance are:  
• Excellent performance: a high level of performance that meets and exceeds norms and expectations and that is 
reflected by substantive indicators of excellence;  
• Effective performance: performance that meets norms and expectations and that is reflected by substantive 
indicators of effective performance;  
• Needs improvement performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of effective performance 
as reflected by substantive indicators of needs improvement performance;  
• Unsatisfactory performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of excellent, effective, and 
needs improvement performance as reflected by substantive indicators of unsatisfactory performance. 
Teaching: 
Your formal teaching assignments included FINC 449 (5 sections), FINC 649 (2 sections), FINC 677, and FINC 689.  You also 
taught FINC 705 for the MS Business program.  Student comments for department courses were overwhelmingly positive, 
with many students describing you as an engaging, knowledgeable, and adaptable instructor who brings energy and clarity 
to complex material. A few students suggested improvements in logistical areas such as timely feedback and assessment 
setup, but these were far outweighed by praise for your teaching effectiveness and classroom environment.

Research and Scholarly Leadership: 
 
As noted above, your position does not have a formal research expectation. However, we are deeply grateful for your 
continued collaboration with the Texas Real Estate Research Center. Your applied research efforts have meaningfully 
advanced the visibility and impact of both the Department of Finance and Mays Business School. By aligning academic 
insight with real-world challenges, you are strengthening the bridge between research and practice, enhancing our 
reputation in the field, and enriching the educational experience for our students. Your dedication reflects a deep 
commitment to service, scholarship, and the broader mission of our college.  
 
Promotion, Tenure, and Reappointment 
Effective September 1, 2024, you were promoted to the rank of Associate Professor of Practice (Executive Associate 
Professor). Congratulations on this wonderful achievement and recognition of your outstanding work. 
 
The Professor of Practice faculty track contains one additional rank: (Full) Professor of Practice (Executive Professor).  
Mays’ promotion guidelines indicate that Associate Professors of Practice (Executive Associate Professors) typically serve 
at least five years in rank before seeking promotion, but that early promotion can be sought. Promotion to Professor of 
Practice (Executive Professor) has the following minimum requirements: 
 
• Pattern over time of excellence in teaching 
• Pattern over time of effective performance in service and/or research and publication 
• Potential for continued excellence in teaching and effective performance in service and/or research and publication 
• Professional conduct conducive to a collegial work environment and standards of professional integrity that will 
advance the interests of Mays Business School and Texas A&M University 
 
I truly appreciate all your contributions to the Adam C. Sinn ’00 Department of Finance and to Mays Business School. It is a 
pleasure to work with you!
