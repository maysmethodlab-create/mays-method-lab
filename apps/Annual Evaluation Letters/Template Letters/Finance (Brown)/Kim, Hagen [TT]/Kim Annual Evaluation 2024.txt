SUBJECT: Annual Performance Review: January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024 
 
Both Texas A&M University and Mays Business School require that the performance of all faculty be reviewed on an 
annual basis.  As noted in the Mays Faculty Promotion and Tenure Guidelines, the purposes of the annual performance 
review include: 
• Creating a sound and logical basis for merit compensation recommendations 
• Providing evaluative feedback regarding how well the individual is currently performing 
• Providing developmental feedback regarding areas where the faculty member’s contribution may be enhanced 
and/or improved in the future 
• Providing feedback regarding progress toward promotion and/or tenure as relevant 
 
Further, the Mays Faculty Promotion and Tenure Guidelines define four levels of performance for the key dimensions 
across which performance is reviewed. These levels of performance are: 
• Excellent performance: a high level of performance that meets and exceeds norms and expectations and that is 
reflected by substantive indicators of excellence; 
• Effective performance: performance that meets norms and expectations and that is reflected by substantive 
indicators of effective performance; 
• Needs improvement performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of effective 
performance as reflected by substantive indicators of needs improvement performance; 
• Unsatisfactory performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of excellent, effective, and 
needs improvement performance as reflected by substantive indicators of unsatisfactory performance. 
 
This letter serves as your performance review for the 2024 calendar year. The following reflects information provided in 
your  Faculty  180  submission.  In  addition, the  tenured  Finance  faculty  had  the  opportunity to  review  your  annual  report 
(containing detailed information about your research, teaching, and service) and current resume and provided me with their 
assessment and feedback.  In the sections below, I first briefly summarize the main contributions you have made in each 
area, present the assessment of the tenured faculty, and provide my own independent assessment.

Research 
Highlights of your research activity during the three-year evaluation window include: 
• In the last three years, you had one publication in the Journal of Corporate Finance.  
• You also have a new revise and resubmit at the Review of Economic Studies, a top Economics journal, in addition 
to three existing papers at the revise and resubmit stage.  
• You also have a new “reject and resubmit” at a top Finance journal.    
 
Teaching 
• You taught one section of FINC 428/660 (Fixed Income Analysis), one section of FINC 489 (Quantitative 
Research Methods in Finance), and one section of FINC 688 (first-year PhD seminar).   
• The design and materials for your courses are at the appropriate level of rigor, and the student evaluations are 
strong.   
 
Service   
• You served as a chair of two dissertation committees.   
• You served on TAMU’s Diversity Fellowship Committee, TAMU’s Graduate Task Force, Mays’s AI Steering 
Committee, and the department’s PhD Program Executive Committee. 
 
Tenured Faculty Assessment 
As you will see below, the committee of professors unanimously rated your research performance as “excellent.” That said, 
the committee members are concerned that you are not converting the papers at the R&R stage into publications quickly 
enough. You were promoted to Associate Professor in 2016, and despite the large number of papers at the R&R stage, you 
have no publications in top Finance or Economics journals since then. To achieve eventual promotion to full professor, it 
will be necessary to push the R&Rs through to publications.    
 
Your teaching performance continues to be excellent (unanimous), and we greatly appreciate your contributions in that area. 
Similarly, we also greatly appreciate your contributions in the service area. Given the comments above about research, we 
encourage  you  to  think  about  how  you  could  reallocate  time  from teaching and  service to  pushing  your research  papers 
through to publication.

Department Head Assessment 
I agree with the assessment and feedback from the  Full Professors. Congratulations  on the  R&R at ReStud! You have a 
deep research pipeline and an impressive number of papers at later (R&R) stages at top journals, but it has been several 
years since you published a paper in a top finance journal. I echo the encouragement from the Full Professors to focus on 
converting these papers as soon as possible. Beyond the implications for promotion to Full Professor, quickly converting 
some of this work is important for you to maintain summer research funding and a three-course teaching load (the current 
policy at Mays requires two “A” publications over the prior 5-year  period  to  keep  summer  funding  and  a  three-course

teaching load). Thus, I strongly support your goal of finalizing some of these R&Rs in 2025, and I hope you will let me 
know if there is anything the department can do to help you reach those goals! 
Thank you for your dedication to teaching, particularly the effort you put into developing the new flex online course for the 
MBA program. I also appreciate your extensive involvement in department and college service, most notably your work 
with PhD students and service on the new college-level AI steering committee. I do agree with recommendation from the

Thank you very much for your contributions to the Adam C. Sinn ’00 Department of Finance and Mays Business School. 
It is a pleasure to work with you!
