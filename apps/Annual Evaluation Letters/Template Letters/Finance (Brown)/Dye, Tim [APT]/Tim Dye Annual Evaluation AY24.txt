SUBJECT: Annual Performance Review: January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024 
 
This letter serves as your performance review for the period January 1, 2024 through December 31, 2024. My evaluation is 
based on the information submitted through Faculty 180, which includes documentation of your teaching performance, 
research productivity, and service activities. 
 
Mays Faculty Promotion and Tenure Guidelines define four levels of performance for the key dimensions across which 
performance is reviewed. These levels of performance are:  
• Excellent performance: a high level of performance that meets and exceeds norms and expectations and that is 
reflected by substantive indicators of excellence;  
• Effective performance: performance that meets norms and expectations and that is reflected by substantive 
indicators of effective performance;  
• Needs improvement performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of effective performance 
as reflected by substantive indicators of needs improvement performance;  
• Unsatisfactory performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of excellent, effective, and 
needs improvement performance as reflected by substantive indicators of unsatisfactory performance. 
 
Teaching: 
 
You delivered an exceptional teaching load this year, leading eight sections across undergraduate and graduate finance 
courses and reaching hundreds of students. Your instruction continues to earn high praise for clarity, energy, and 
relevance, and your student evaluations consistently highlight both your teaching effectiveness and the care you show for 
students’ well-being. In addition to teaching core courses such as FINC 351, 603, and 632, your long-term service across 
flagship programs—including Aggies on Wall Street, Tanner Fund, PPA, Commercial Banking, MS Business, and the Full-
Time MBA—demonstrates the breadth and depth of your impact. 
You are a clear model of pedagogical excellence and leadership. Over your 27-year career at Mays, you have taught 14 
different courses and consistently excelled in diverse instructional settings. Your work to integrate Bloomberg Professional 
Service™, develop portfolio management content using Reliant Center resources, and create innovative instructional tools 
like Excel-based interactive modules for portfolio theory showcases your commitment to equipping students with 
practical, differentiating skills. Your willingness to mentor colleagues and share teaching resources further amplifies your 
influence.  
Your formal teaching assignments included FINC 351 (2 sections), FINC 603 (2 sections), and FINC 632 (4 sections). Course 
evaluation comments were generally positive, with many students indicating that you are a highly knowledgeable, 
passionate, and engaging instructor who explains complex concepts clearly and fosters an effective learning environment. 
Others noted occasional concerns regarding alignment between lecture content and assessments, as well as the level of 
challenge presented on exams relative to in-class examples. Overall, your teaching is highly valued, with students 
frequently ranking you among the best instructors they’ve had at Mays.

Service: 
 
Your service to the department this year has been outstanding.  Your long-standing support of key programs like BBA-
Finance, MSF, and student groups such as the Financial Services Organization and Orthodox Christian Fellowship shows 
how invested you are in our students’ success both in and out of the classroom. 
 
Behind the scenes, your work on course scheduling and curricular management has been a significant help to the

Research and Scholarly Leadership: 
 
The research expectations for Clinical faculty members vary based on individual preferences and departmental needs. In 
addition to teaching responsibilities, Clinical faculty are expected to engage in a combination of service and research 
activities. A Clinical faculty who actively engages in research would be expected to have a lower level of service activity, 
and vice versa.   
  
Reviewing your previous performance evaluations, it appears that research does not seem to be one of your career 
objectives at this time.
