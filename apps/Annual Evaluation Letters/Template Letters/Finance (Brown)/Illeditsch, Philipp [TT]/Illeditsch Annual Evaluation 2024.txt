SUBJECT: Annual Performance Review: January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024 
 
Both Texas A&M University and Mays Business School require that the performance of all faculty be reviewed on an 
annual basis.  As noted in the Mays Faculty Promotion and Tenure Guidelines, the purposes of the annual performance 
review include: 
• Creating a sound and logical basis for merit compensation recommendations 
• Providing evaluative feedback regarding how well the individual is currently performing 
• Providing developmental feedback regarding areas where the faculty member’s contribution may be 
enhanced and/or improved in the future 
• Providing feedback regarding progress toward promotion and/or tenure as relevant 
 
Further, the Mays Faculty Promotion and Tenure Guidelines define four levels of performance for the key dimensions 
across which performance is reviewed. These levels of performance are: 
• Excellent performance: a high level of performance that meets and exceeds norms and expectations and that is 
reflected by substantive indicators of excellence; 
• Effective performance: performance that meets norms and expectations and that is reflected by substantive 
indicators of effective performance; 
• Needs improvement performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of effective 
performance as reflected by substantive indicators of needs improvement performance; 
• Unsatisfactory performance: performance that falls below norms and expectations of excellent, effective, and 
needs improvement performance as reflected by substantive indicators of unsatisfactory performance. 
 
This letter serves as your performance review for the 2024 calendar year. The following reflects information provided in 
your  Faculty  180  submission.  In  addition, the  tenured  Finance  faculty  had  the  opportunity to  review  your  annual  report 
(containing detailed information about your research, teaching, and service) and current resume and provided me with their 
assessment and feedback.  In the sections below, I first briefly summarize the main contributions you have made in each 
area, present the assessment of the tenured faculty, and provide my own independent assessment.

Research 
Highlights of your research activity during the three-year evaluation window include: 
• In the last three years, you had no publications or acceptances of research papers.  
• You produced new working papers and received another revise-and-resubmit request from the Journal of Financial 
Economics, a top three journal in Finance.  
• You submitted multiple working papers to top journals in Finance and Economics.  
• You were active in Finance conferences as both discussant and presenter. 
 
Teaching 
• You taught one section of FINC 351 (Investment Analysis), one section of FINC 688 (first-year PhD seminar), and 
one section of FINC 689 (Stochastic Methods in Finance).  
• The design and materials for your courses are at the appropriate level of rigor, and the student evaluations are 
strong.  
 
Service   
• You served as primary grader for the first-year qualifying exam for the PhD students.  
• You provided service to the profession through refereeing papers for top journals and discussing papers at 
conferences.  
• You served as a member of two dissertation committees.  
 
Tenured Faculty Assessment 
As  you  will  see  below, the tenured faculty unanimously rated your research performance as “needs improvement.” The 
reason for the rating is that you must go up for promotion and tenure this coming year, and you have no new acceptances 
of papers at top journals in your probationary period. We strongly encourage you to resubmit your paper to the JFE as soon 
as possible to try to secure an acceptance. Although time is very short, to the extent possible, we also strongly encourage 
you to advance other papers to the R&R or acceptance stage.  
Your teaching performance continues to be excellent (unanimous), and we greatly appreciate your contributions in that area. 
Similarly, we  also  greatly appreciate  your contributions  in the  service area, in particular your contributions to the  Ph.D. 
program.

Department Head Assessment 
I agree with the assessment of the tenured faculty. Your teaching and service contributions are excellent. Notably, you serve 
as  a  referee  for  numerous  top  journals  and  conferences  and are  in  exceedingly  high  demand  as  discussant,  all  of  which 
speaks to your reputation and high standing in the field.  
Your research trajectory is a concern given how close we are to your mandatory tenure review. You have several promising 
research projects at various stages of completion, and it is important for you to move these forward as fast as possible given 
your  mandatory  tenure  review  in  Fall  2025.  Toward  this  end,  I  applaud  your  goal  of  strengthening  your  tenure  case  by 
publishing the JFE paper and submitting multiple other working papers to top journals by the end of the summer. It will be 
challenging to get one of the working papers all the way through the acceptance process before your tenure review, but even 
an  R&R  will  show research  promise  and  strengthen  your  case.  If  there  is  something  the  department  can  do  to  help  you 
achieve these goals, I hope you will let me know! 
I  am  particularly  grateful  for  your  service  contributions  to  the  department,  particularly  your  engagement  with  the  PhD 
program. 
Thank you very much for your contributions to the Adam C. Sinn ’00 Department of Finance and Mays Business School. 
It is a pleasure to work with you!
