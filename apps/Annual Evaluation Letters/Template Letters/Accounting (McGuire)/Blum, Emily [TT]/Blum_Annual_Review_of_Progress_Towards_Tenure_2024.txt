SUBJECT:	Annual Review of Progress Towards Tenure



Thank you for submitting your annual Professional Activity Report. This evaluation covers your performance in research, teaching, and service from January 1, 2024, to December 31, 2024. Following Mays Business School Faculty Guidelines, this annual review is conducted by the tenured faculty of the department and performance is assessed using the categories of excellent, effective, needs improvement, and unsatisfactory.



The tenured faculty thoughtfully reviewed your annual report and vita. Their insights into your accomplishments and progress towards tenure serve as an important input to my evaluation. This assessment of your accomplishments, considered within the context of our school and department goals, informs both departmental resource allocation and your annual reappointment as an assistant professor.



I remain grateful for your work ethic and collegiality. You have added to the research culture in our department through your active participation in workshops and through informal discussions with faculty colleagues and PhD students. It is both notable and very exciting that had two studies conditionally accepted for publication this year, one in The Accounting Review and another in Contemporary Accounting Research. It is also noteworthy that you have received two third round revise and resubmit decisions, one at The Accounting Review and the other at Accounting, Organizations and Society. It is exciting to see these projects consistently moving through the review process and it demonstrates how hard you have been working. The tenured faculty and I strongly encourage you to continue to focus on moving your research projects forward. In additional feedback, they suggested that you begin to establish a pipeline of new working papers that are co-authored with colleagues from outside your current co-author set. It is also very positive that you were present at a peer school this past calendar year. Overall, based on the Mays Guidelines, your research performance is effective.



ACCT 327 is a very important course, and I appreciate your conscientious teaching efforts. As we have discussed this year, your teaching evaluations for ACCT 327 in Spring 2024 were below our departmental norms and represent an area for growth. I think your evaluations were partially a function of a low response rate, which was below the university’s expectations (75 percent), which suggests that they may not reflect the full scope of your teaching efforts. It's commendable how proactively you collaborated with your ACCT 327 colleagues this past fall to incorporate new elements aimed at strengthening both the course and student feedback. Please continue this valuable collaboration and don't hesitate to seek advice from experienced instructors. I appreciate your dedication and look forward to seeing the impact of these efforts both in the short- and long-run. Based on the Mays Guidelines, your teaching for the 2024 calendar year is assessed as needs improvement. Although it is not part of your 2024 annual review, I was delighted to see that your teaching evaluations for spring 2025 demonstrated substantial improvement and are in line with departmental norms. Thank you for the significant effort that you made to enhance the student experience in your courses.

We, of course, want your service activities to be limited at this time in your career, and I believe that it is appropriate that you are serving on the PhD committee and involved in the PhD program. I appreciate you continuing to make yourself available to meet with and/or go to a meal with workshop visitors, faculty recruits, and prospective PhD students. Based on the Mays Guidelines, your service is effective.



As you know, I met with the tenured faculty to discuss the progress of assistant professors toward tenure. Based upon a review of your Professional Activity and Accomplishment Report and your vita, the consensus of the tenured faculty is that you are on trajectory toward achieving a tenurable record. The consensus was that your success in publishing your work in the top scholarly journals and your teaching success will be critical to a positive tenure decision. I concur with the overall evaluation of the committee and with your current goals. I encourage you to continue to focus on publishing impactful research in our top journals and will support you however I can. 



I appreciate your efforts toward achieving your goals and the goals of our department, and I hope you have an enjoyable and productive year ahead. Please let me know if you have any questions about my assessment and contact Diana Kruse if you would like to schedule a time to meet with me. Thank you again for everything that you do for our department. I am grateful to be your colleague!
