SUBJECT:	Annual Review





Thank you for submitting your annual Professional Activity and Accomplishment Report this spring. The purpose of this memo is to provide you with my assessment of your performance from January 1, 2024 to December 31, 2024. My assessment is based upon the Mays Guidelines and will be the basis for any department resource allocation decisions and for the reappointment decision. Following the Mays Guidelines, the performance categories are excellent, effective, needs improvement, and unsatisfactory.



Similar to last year, I cannot express how much I appreciate your dedication to teaching and student success. Your impact on our students is tremendous and will shape their careers. Your collegiality, positivity, optimism, and your love of teaching are infectious and are a tremendous asset in our department. You teach multiple important classes for our department, and your student evaluations are very positive across the board. You teach one of our very important Intermediate Accounting courses (ACCT 328) and you teach Energy Accounting and the Energy Seminar (ACCT 403/603) as well. I also appreciate that you are willing to teach in the summer! It is great for our students and very helpful for the department. 



I also greatly appreciate you developing and teaching ACCT 602 in our Flex Online program. I recognize the significant time that the Flex Online development process takes in addition to the time spent teaching the course. I am very grateful that you are willing to be such a significant part of the Flex Online program. It is very impactful to have our best faculty involved in the program. 



Your leadership over the Energy Accounting Certificate (EAC) program and development of the Energy Accounting Advisory Board are incredible and very impactful. The growth in the program under your leadership remains one of the most exciting and important initiatives in our department. I also am so grateful for the way you invest specifically in each student, celebrate their success, and help them achieve their career objectives. I firmly believe the EAC program and Advisory Board will continue to thrive under your leadership and I look forward to helping in any way that I can.



I am very grateful for your willingness to lead the Bahr Petroleum Ventures Program. The growth in the program under your leadership has been astounding. PVP is critical for the success of our students, department, and college. I am looking forward to continuing to help you however I can. Thank you!



I also appreciate your various service activities, including creating high-impact experiences for our students through PVP, EAC (e.g., field trip to Haliburton) and recruiting students to the accounting major. I also appreciate how you represent Mays and our department at various energy-related conferences and your involvement in the McFerrin Center for Entrepreneurship. I am also very grateful for your service on the James Benjamin Department of Accounting Advisory Council (thank you!). 



AACSB guidelines for accreditation maintenance specify that all faculty are expected to demonstrate activities that maintain the currency and relevance of their instruction, and that “all accounting faculty demonstrate sufficient ongoing professional interaction to support their role in achieving the unit’s mission.” While your current activities seem more than sufficient, I urge you to be proactive in identifying appropriate activities in the future to maintain the instructional practice status for accreditation. 



Based on the Mays Guidelines, I assess both your teaching and service as excellent. I am very thankful to be your colleague and am very grateful for the tremendous impact that you are having on our students. Please let me know if you have any questions about my assessment. To that end, please contact Diana Kruse to schedule a time to meet with me if you would like to discuss my assessment. Thank you for everything that you do for our department!
