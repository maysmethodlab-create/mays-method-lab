This memorandum includes our recommendation for Dr. Steve Boivie to be reappointed to the Carroll & Dorothy Conn Chair in New Ventures Leadership for a five (5) year term.

The Mays Research Council unanimously supported the reappointment of Dr. Boivie to the Carroll & Dorothy Conn Chair in New Ventures Leadership for a five (5) year term, citing his exceptional research productivity, leadership in the field of corporate governance, and sustained scholarly impact as one of the most published scholars in top-tier journals.

Post-Tenure Review
At Mays Business School the post-tenure review of faculty members serving in endowed position takes place during the review process for the endowed position. By school policy, a successful renewal of the endowed position is deemed to be a successful outcome of the post-tenure review process.

Review Process
At Mays Business School, the Mays Research Council considers all nominees for appointment and reappointment to endowed positions and provides recommendations to the Associate Dean for Research and Scholarship who, in turn, provides those recommendations to the Dean.

Members of the Mays Research Council serve three-year terms. The Dean appoints individuals upon acceptance of a recommendation from the Department Head and the Senior Associate Dean. One faculty member from each of our five academic departments serves as a member of the Council. Those eligible to serve are full professors in the department who hold an endowed chair. Faculty serving in administrative positions such as Department Head, Associate Dean, or Dean are not eligible to serve, except in ex-officio roles without voting rights.

The members of the Mays Research Council reviewed each recommendation and provided their own independent assessment by secret ballot using a Qualtrics survey before the meeting. Council members had the same choices as those available to the candidate's immediate supervisor. Specifically, for candidates serving in an endowed professorship, Council members chose between reappointment at the endowed professorship level, first-time appointment to an endowed chair, and non-renewal. For candidates serving in an endowed chair, Council members chose between renewal at the chair level, appointment to a professorship instead of a chair, and non-reappointment to any type of endowed position. In addition, we provided Council members with an opportunity to enter additional comments (also anonymously) for consideration by the Dean.

Candidate's Achievement and Qualifications
The members of the Mays Research Council unanimously concurred with the department head's recommendation for Dr. Steve Boivie, citing his exceptional research productivity, scholarly impact, and leadership in the field of management. Over the past five years, Dr. Boivie has published 12 articles—9 of which appeared in premier journals such as AMJ, ASQ, and SMJ—placing him among the top scholars in the discipline. His work has garnered over 5,600 citations, with more than half coming in the last five years, and his research has been featured in over 50 media outlets. These accomplishments reflect both scholarly rigor and practical relevance. Dr. Boivie's record firmly establishes him as a leader in management research and an outstanding choice for renewal to the Carroll & Dorothy Conn Chair in New Ventures Leadership.
